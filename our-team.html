Rebecca Kasika

Founding Member

Rebecca Kasika is a Tanzanian Social Worker and Public Health Specialist with over 11 years of experience designing and managing community‑based programs that strengthen local health systems and protect vulnerable populations.

She holds an MSc in Public Health from Muhimbili University of Health and Allied Sciences and a BA in Social Work & Social Administration from Bugema University and has completed executive training at the Humphrey School of Public Affairs (University of Minnesota) as well as the Mandela Washington Fellowship (U.S. Department of State).

Fluent in Kiswahili and English (with basic French), Rebecca has forged partnerships across the Ministry of Health, PORALG, Ministry of Education, and national school‑ and reproductive‑health programs to embed child eye health into policy and practice. At the Brien Holden Vision Institute, she led integrated eye‑health initiatives across 14 districts---training over 75,000 teachers and frontline health workers and directly benefiting more than 1.4 million children.

Today, Rebecca serves as Programme Administrator for Quality Assurance at the Embassy of Sweden in Dar es Salaam. In this role, she ensures full compliance with Sida's operational standards, oversees financial stewardship and internal controls for all contributions and builds the capacity of program officers and decision‑makers. She coordinates international training courses---having trained 300+ Tanzanian government leaders---manages multi‑million‑dollar budgets and contributes to appraisal and learning processes across the Embassy's portfolio.

Driven by a passion for inclusive economic empowerment, human rights, and sustainable development, Rebecca continues to champion social justice and equitable access to health and education in Tanzania.

Christina Sifael Mbise

Founding Member | Treasurer

Christina Sifael Mbise is a seasoned public health professional and one of the founding members of the Vision Care Foundation (VCF). With over a decade of extensive experience in public health and development programming, she has played a pivotal role in advancing eye health systems and promoting inclusive, sustainable health services in Tanzania.

Ms. Mbise holds a master's degree in public health from the University of Dar es Salaam, complemented by a Postgraduate Diploma in Leadership and Management of the Civil Society Sector from Strathmore University in Kenya, and a Bachelor of Arts in Sociology from the University of Dar es Salaam. Her strong academic background has provided a solid foundation for her strategic leadership in program design, implementation, policy advocacy, and capacity building.

Professionally, she has held key technical and managerial roles in several international NGOs, including Sightsavers, Pact, JHPIEGO, FHI360, and Amref Health Africa. She has led major eye health initiatives, including the integration of primary eye care into the national health system, the development of an Eye Health Information System, and the inclusion of eye health commodities into Tanzania's Medical Stores Department (MSD) supply chain.

Within Vision Care Foundation, Ms. Mbise continues to provide strategic leadership and technical oversight, particularly in aligning the Foundation's programs with national priorities and global frameworks such as the 2030 In Sight strategy. Her commitment to evidence-based approaches, inclusive health services, and system strengthening is instrumental in achieving VCF's vision of equitable and quality eye care for all.

Public Health Specialist | Eye Health Systems Advocate | Strategic Program Leader

Dr. Frank Sandi, MD, FRCOph(ECSA)

Founding Member | Past President - Tanzania Ophthalmology Society

Dr. Frank Sandi is a highly respected ophthalmologist, academician, and public health advocate with a distinguished career dedicated to advancing eye health in Tanzania and the broader Eastern, Central, and Southern Africa region. As one of the founding members of the Vision Care Foundation (VCF), Dr. Sandi has been instrumental in establishing and implementing innovative, community-based eye care interventions targeting vulnerable and underserved populations.

With advanced clinical training in glaucoma, cataract surgery (including PHACO techniques), and neuro-ophthalmology, Dr. Sandi brings a wealth of clinical expertise to the Foundation. He is a Fellow of the College of Ophthalmology of Eastern, Central and Southern Africa (COECSA) and a graduate of prestigious leadership development programs under the American Academy of Ophthalmology, International Council of Ophthalmology, and African Ophthalmology Council.

Dr. Sandi also holds a Diploma in Tropical Medicine and Hygiene from the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine and a Postgraduate Diploma in Community Economic Development, which reflects his broader commitment to public health and socioeconomic equity in healthcare delivery.

In his current roles as Head of the Department of Surgery at the University of Dodoma (UDOM) and Clinical Instructor at Benjamin Mkapa Hospital, Dr. Sandi actively mentors the next generation of eye health professionals while leading large-scale outreach programs. His leadership has facilitated vision screening and surgical restoration services for thousands of Tanzanians, in partnership with global institutions such as Weill Cornell Medicine and the Moran Eye Center.

He also served as President of the Tanzania Ophthalmology Society, where he champions national and regional eye health policy, and as Trachoma Technical Advisor to the Ministry of Health, supporting efforts to eliminate neglected tropical diseases causing blindness.

Dr. Sandi's pioneering work with the Vision Care Foundation continues to drive impactful, evidence-based interventions that aim to eliminate avoidable blindness and ensure equitable access to quality eye care across Tanzania.

Careen Erick Mashayo

Founding Member | Licensed Optometrist

Careen Erick is a licensed and accomplished optometrist with over 15 years of experience in clinical eye care, public health, and community vision programs. As a founding member of Vision Care Foundation, she has played a vital role in designing and delivering sustainable eye health interventions aimed at improving the lives of underserved communities across Tanzania.

She serves as a National Trainer of Trainers (TOT) for School Eye Health, a position through which she has successfully trained over 50 trainers across various regions. Her trainees --- including teachers, health professionals, and community facilitators --- have become active agents of change in identifying, managing, and referring school children with visual impairments. This work directly supports the broader national agenda for child eye health and aligns with the 2030 In Sight global strategy.

Careen is a graduate of KCMC School of Optometry, and her core competencies include comprehensive eye examinations, prescription of corrective lenses, patient education, and managing outreach programs. She has been at the forefront of school screenings and vision care campaigns in Lindi, Mtwara, Simiyu, Kilimanjaro, and other regions.

As a passionate advocate for quality, accessible eye care, Careen has represented Vision Care Foundation at both national and international platforms, including the IAPB Council of Members and the All Eye Health Scientific Congress. She is also a proud, active member of the Tanzania Optometric Association (TOA).

Careen's leadership, compassion, and technical expertise make her a powerful force in the fight against avoidable blindness in Tanzania.
